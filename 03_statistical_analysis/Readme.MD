**03 – Statistical Validation of COVID-19 Metrics**
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Objective -**

• Perform statistical validation of pandemic indicators to assess severity, growth dynamics, and inter-variable relationships before forecasting implementation.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Analytical Scope -**

• Case Fatality Rate (CFR) computation

• Population-normalized infection rate

• Daily growth rate analysis

• Correlation and statistical significance testing

• Distribution behavior (Skewness & Kurtosis)

• Volatility assessment

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Key Findings -**

• Strong positive correlation observed between new cases and new deaths.

• Case fatality rate varies across states, indicating heterogeneous severity.

• Infection rate normalization provides more accurate cross-state comparison.

• Daily growth rate confirms non-linear expansion phases.

• Distribution of new cases is right-skewed with high kurtosis, reflecting surge behavior.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Outcome -** Established statistically validated foundation for downstream modeling and forecasting by quantifying severity, growth behavior, and dependency structure.
